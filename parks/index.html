Parks & the Outdoors

The Mohawk Valley is greener than people expect — a historic Olmsted park system in the city, a canal trail running through it, and the southern Adirondacks out the back door. Here's where to start.

🌳 The Olmsted parks

Utica is the smallest city in the country with a park system designed by Frederick Law Olmsted (the mind behind Central Park).

  • Roscoe Conkling Park — the big one, home to the Utica Zoo, the Memorial Parkway, and sledding hills in winter.
  • F.T. Proctor & T.R. Proctor Parks — formal gardens, tennis, and the lily pond.
  • The Memorial Parkway — a tree-lined boulevard built for strolling and biking.

🚲 Trails & water

You're on the Erie Canal — and the trail that runs beside it.

  • Erie Canalway Trail — flat, paved, and scenic; ride or walk it for miles right through the Valley.
  • Utica Marsh — a wildlife management area with boardwalks and birding minutes from downtown.
  • The Mohawk River & Barge Canal — paddling and the new Harbor Point waterfront in Utica.

⛰️ State parks & day trips

A short drive opens up lakes, falls, and mountains.

  • Delta Lake State Park (Rome) — swimming, camping, and a big reservoir.
  • Pixley Falls (Boonville) — an easy waterfall hike north of Rome.
  • Verona Beach State Park — a sandy beach on Oneida Lake.
  • The Adirondacks — Old Forge and the Fulton Chain of Lakes are your gateway to the north woods.

❄️ When it snows

The Valley leans into winter.

  • Val Bialas Ski Center (Utica) — a city ski hill and skating rink.
  • Cross-country skiing and snowshoeing on the canal trail and in the parks.
