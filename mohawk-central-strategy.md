# Mohawk Central — Strategy & Roadmap
### The front page of the Mohawk Valley
*Prepared June 2026*

---

## The one-line idea

**Mohawk Central is the single place you check to know what's worth your time in the Mohawk Valley** — a curated, magazine-style front page mixing events, food, and local headlines, with a free weekly email as the engine that pulls people back.

Not a newspaper. Not a calendar buried on a tourism site. A friendly, opinionated, *curated* front page — the local equivalent of "what's good this week," run lean.

---

## Why this can work right now

Three things are true about the Valley in 2026:

1. **The legacy outlets leave a gap.** WKTV (NBC affiliate) does broadcast news; the Observer-Dispatch is a shrinking Gannett daily behind a paywall; the Rome Sentinel covers Rome; tourism sites are static. **Nobody owns the warm, curated, "here's what to do and where to go this week" lane** — delivered by email, free, and fun to read.
2. **The audience is growing and changing.** The $1B+ Wolfspeed chip fab at Marcy and its supplier ecosystem are bringing in newcomers who know nothing about the area and want orientation. The Nexus Center pulls 360,000+ visitors a year. Harbor Point is reshaping the waterfront. New money, new people, new attention.
3. **The local-newsletter playbook is proven.** Lean local email brands (the 6AM City model, Naptown Scoop, etc.) have shown a one-person curated newsletter can reach tens of thousands and sell local sponsorships profitably. The Valley doesn't have one yet.

**The bet:** be first and be consistent. In local media, the brand that reliably shows up every week wins the habit — and habit is the moat.

## The product

### The website (the curated front page)
A magazine-style homepage updated weekly, organized in clear lanes:

- **This Week** — the headline events, top of the page, always fresh.
- **Eat & Drink** — openings, closings, the food worth driving for (riggies, greens, tomato pie, the brewery scene).
- **The Valley** — 3–5 curated local headlines (sourced and linked to WKTV, O-D, Rome Sentinel, etc. — you curate, you don't have to report).
- **Do** — evergreen guides that rarely change: best hikes, the Olmsted parks, day trips, "new to the Valley? start here."
- **Games** — Comets, Nexus Center tournaments, City FC in season.

### The newsletter (the engine)
The free weekly email *is* the growth machine — it's what people subscribe to, forward, and return from. Every issue drives traffic back to the front page. **This already exists and is scheduled (Mondays).**

### The key efficiency
**One research pass feeds both.** The weekly digest research → becomes the email → becomes the week's homepage refresh. You are doing the work once. That's what keeps this inside "a few hours a week."

## How it stays low-effort (the operating model)

| Cadence | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly (Mon)** | Review the auto-drafted digest, edit, send. Same content updates the homepage "This Week" + "The Valley." | ~1–2 hrs |
| **Monthly** | Refresh an evergreen "Do" guide; check sponsor slots. | ~1 hr |
| **As-needed** | Reply to subscriber emails, onboard a sponsor. | minimal |

The automation already in place (the scheduled Monday digest) does the heavy lifting of *gathering*. Your job is editorial judgment and voice — the part that can't be automated and the part that builds the brand.

## How it makes money

Revenue scales with list size and habit. Rough phased model:

1. **Local sponsorships (primary).** One sponsor per weekly email + a homepage banner. Local newsletters typically sell a weekly sponsor slot for **~$150–$500** depending on list size. Even a 1,500-subscriber list with an engaged open rate is sellable to a brewery, realtor, dealership, or law firm. Goal: 1 recurring sponsor by ~1,000 subscribers.
2. **Directory / featured listings.** Restaurants and shops pay a small monthly fee ($25–$100) for an enhanced listing. Stacks on top of sponsorships; low effort once the template exists.
3. **Classifieds / events submission fee.** Let businesses pay to feature an event. Self-serve, passive.
4. **Affiliate & merch (side income).** Brewery tours, ticket links, and the "Made in the Mohawk Valley" merch idea (half-moon cookies, riggies kits, Utica Club gear) for the diaspora.

**The math that matters:** this is a list-size game. Sponsorship rate scales with subscribers, so the first job is *audience*, not revenue. Don't sell ads to 200 people; grow to a few thousand engaged locals first.

## Growth plan (getting to audience)

- **Seed (now):** the live teaser page is collecting emails pre-launch. Good.
- **Launch:** first real Monday issue. Ask every subscriber to forward to one person ("know someone who'd want this?") — built into the welcome email already.
- **Local partnerships:** get the signup link in front of existing audiences — the chamber, Nexus Center, breweries, "new hire" packets at big employers (Wolfspeed, hospitals, MVHS), Facebook community groups.
- **Newcomer wedge:** a "New to the Mohawk Valley? Start here" page is irresistible to relocating chip-plant families and ranks well in search. This is your highest-leverage single page.
- **Referral mechanic:** later, a simple "refer 3 friends" reward via the email tool.

## What to measure

- **Subscribers** (the headline number — drives everything)
- **Open rate** (>40% is healthy for local; it's the proof of habit you sell to sponsors)
- **Forward / referral rate** (organic growth signal)
- **Homepage return visits**
- **Sponsor revenue** (lagging indicator — comes after audience)

---

## The honest risks

- **Consistency.** The whole model rests on showing up every week. The automation de-risks this, but the send still needs you. Miss weeks and the habit dies.
- **Curation discipline.** "Front page of the Valley" works because it's *edited*. Resist becoming a firehose; the value is what you leave out.
- **Time creep.** If it starts eating more than a few hours, lean harder on automation and evergreen content rather than adding daily obligations.

---

## Bottom line

You already own the three hardest things to get: a perfect domain, a live site, and an automated engine. The strategy is simply: **show up every Monday, be the warm curated voice nobody else is, grow the list relentlessly, and let sponsorship revenue follow the audience.** Be first, be consistent, and the Valley's habit becomes your moat.
