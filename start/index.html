For newcomers

New to the Mohawk Valley? Start here.

Whether you moved for a job at the Marcy chip campus, a hospital, a school, or just landed here — welcome. Here's a friendly, no-nonsense orientation to the Utica–Rome area from people who live it.

📍 Where you are

The Mohawk Valley sits in the middle of New York State, strung along the Mohawk River and the historic Erie Canal, halfway between Albany and Syracuse on the Thruway.

  • Utica — the hub: the biggest city, the food, the arts, the Nexus Center, and the Saranac brewery.
  • Rome — to the west: Revolutionary-era history (Fort Stanwix), Griffiss business/tech park, and a strong community feel.
  • New Hartford, Whitesboro, New York Mills, Clinton — the suburbs and villages just outside Utica, popular with families.
  • Herkimer County & the river towns — Ilion, Mohawk, Herkimer, Little Falls and the smaller school towns to the east.
  • Marcy — home to the Wolfspeed silicon-carbide chip fab and SUNY Poly, the area's fast-growing tech corner.

🍝 Eat like you grew up here

The Valley has a food identity all its own. Try these and you'll fit right in:

  • Chicken riggies — rigatoni in a spicy-creamy tomato sauce with cherry peppers. The unofficial regional dish.
  • Utica greens — escarole sautéed with peppers, prosciutto, and breadcrumbs.
  • Tomato pie — square, sauce-on-top, served room-temperature (Roma and O'Scugnizzo are institutions).
  • Half-moon cookies — the local cousin of the black-and-white, frosted half chocolate, half vanilla.
  • Utica Club & Saranac — brewed in the city since 1888 at the F.X. Matt Brewery; the tour is a rite of passage.

🚗 Getting around

  • By car: the NYS Thruway (I-90) runs right through; most of the Valley is a 15–25 minute drive end to end.
  • By air: Syracuse (SYR) is about an hour west; Albany (ALB) about 90 minutes east.
  • By rail: Amtrak stops at Utica's historic Union Station.
  • Day trips: you're at the doorstep of the Adirondacks to the north, Cooperstown and the Baseball Hall of Fame to the south, and Turning Stone Resort just west.

🎟️ Things to do

  • Munson (MWPAI) — a genuinely world-class art museum, free to visit.
  • The Stanley Theatre — a restored 1928 movie palace hosting Broadway tours and concerts.
  • Nexus Center & Adirondack Bank Center — Utica Comets hockey, tournaments, and big events downtown.
  • Olmsted parks — Utica is the smallest city with a Frederick Law Olmsted-designed park system (Roscoe Conkling and F.T. Proctor parks).
  • The Erie Canalway Trail — bike or walk the canal path right through the Valley.
  • The Boilermaker — the famous 15K road race every July, with the legendary post-race party at the brewery.

👨‍👩‍👧 For families & movers

The area's schools span big districts and small village schools — many stream their games, concerts, and graduations, which you can catch on our Catch Up replay archive and the school streams board.

Big local employers include the Wolfspeed chip fab at Marcy, Mohawk Valley Health System and area hospitals, the school districts, and the Griffiss park employers in Rome.
