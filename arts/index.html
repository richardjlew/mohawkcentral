Arts & Nights Out

For its size, the Valley has a serious culture scene — a baroque movie palace, a world-class art museum, brewery concerts, and a downtown that's waking back up. Here's where to go.

🎭 The big stages

Two anchors carry most of the marquee programming.

  • The Stanley Theatre — a restored 1928 Mexican-baroque movie palace on Genesee St, home to the Broadway Theatre League's touring shows and major concerts.
  • Munson (MWPAI) — a genuinely world-class art museum (free to visit), plus a Performing Arts series and the Great Artists concert series.

🎶 Live music

From brewery lawns to downtown bars.

  • Saranac / F.X. Matt Brewery — summer concerts and the 1888 Tavern stage.
  • Adirondack Bank Center & Nexus Center — arena concerts, Utica Comets hockey, and big events downtown.
  • Turning Stone (Verona) — a showroom and event center pulling national acts.

🎟️ Theater & symphony

Local companies keep the stage busy year-round.

  • Players of Utica and area community theaters.
  • The Mohawk Valley's orchestral & chamber concerts through Munson and regional ensembles.

🍸 A night downtown

Bagg's Square and Varick St (“The Brewery District”) are the cores of Utica nightlife — breweries, restaurants, and bars within a walk of each other, plus a growing First-Friday gallery scene.
